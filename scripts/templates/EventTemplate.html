# Structure

Round 1:

A Multiple choice questions(MCQ) round. Time allotted is 30 minutes. Negative marking will be implemented. Further details will be informed to the team at the time of event.

Round 2:

The top teams from the Round 1 will qualify for this round. This round will be a rapid coding round where the team has to complete the codes within the stipulated time. The output file needs to be submitted and will be judged on the basis of the time taken to code it. Further details will be informed to the team at the time of event.

Round 3:

The top teams from Round 2 will qualify for this round. This round will also be a rapid coding round and relatively difficult questions will be set up. The code must be working as well as efficient. All the possible inputs and corner cases should be handled. The codes will be judged on the basis of efficiency of the code and the time required to code it.

Elimination Criteria:

1st round: Groups of two to have MCQ test 2nd round: Top 10 teams Qualify for round two 3rd round: Top 5 teams for round 3

# Rules

  1. The environment will be either Ubuntu 14.04 OS with gedit/vim/emacs as text editor and gcc as compiler OR Windows 7 with Turbo C / Dev C++ (as per the team's choice).
  2. Participants need to bring their College ID cards and the receipt of registration during reporting.
  3. Participants are expected to give their correct contact details, so as to inform them about the results.
  4. Rules may be changed without prior intimation. Participants are requested to check the MindSpark'22 website (www.mind-spark.org) regularly for updates.
  5. In case of any disputes, the decision of the organizers and the judges will be
    6. final and binding to all.
  6. Team members may be from different colleges/ institutions but one participant may not be a part of multiple teams for the same event.

# FAQs

What is the team structure?

A team may consist of maximum 2 participants. The members of the team can be from different educational institutions but should be strictly First Year or Second Year Undergraduates.

Do I have to bring anything?

College ID Cards and receipt of registration are compulsory; you may carry pen or pencil, if you wish to work out a rough plan for rounds 2 and 3.

Which IDE (Integrated Development Environment) will be provided?

The environment provided will be Ubuntu 14.04 OS with emacs/vim/gedit as text editor and gcc as compiler or Windows 7 OS with Turbo C/ Dev C++, depending on choice of the participating teams. COEP recommends Linux.
